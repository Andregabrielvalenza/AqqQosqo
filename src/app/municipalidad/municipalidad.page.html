Get to know us

Welcome to Cusco! One of the most beautiful and historically rich cities in South America. As your online tour guide, let me introduce you to the fascinating culture and landmarks of this magnificent city. Cusco is the former capital of the Inca Empire and is home to some of the most impressive ancient ruins in the world. One of the must-see landmarks in the city is the iconic Machu Picchu. This ancient Incan city is perched on a mountain top and can be reached by taking a scenic train ride through the Andes Mountains. Once you arrive, you will be struck by the sheer beauty of the ruins, which have been perfectly preserved for centuries. Apart from Machu Picchu, there are many other historical landmarks in the city worth exploring. The Plaza de Armas is the main square and the heart of the city. Here, you will find the stunning Cusco Cathedral, which was built in the 16th century and features a beautiful blend of Spanish and Incan architecture. Another must-visit landmark is the Inca ruins of Sacsayhuaman, which was once a fortress and features massive stone walls and towers. Cusco is also a great place to experience traditional Peruvian culture. The city is known for its vibrant festivals, which are celebrated throughout the year. The Inti Raymi festival, which takes place in June, is one of the most popular and features a colorful parade and traditional Incan ceremonies. Additionally, Cusco is home to many great restaurants where you can try traditional Peruvian dishes such as ceviche, lomo saltado, and aji de gallina. If you enjoy outdoor activities, Cusco is the perfect place to explore. The city is surrounded by the Andes Mountains, which offer some of the best hiking and trekking opportunities in the world. One of the most popular treks is the Inca Trail, which takes you through breathtaking scenery and ends at Machu Picchu. Finally, Cusco is a city with a rich arts and crafts scene. You will find many shops and markets selling handmade goods such as alpaca wool clothing, silver jewelry, and traditional pottery. A visit to the San Blas neighborhood is a must for those interested in seeing the works of local artisans. In conclusion, Cusco is a city that has something for everyone. Whether you are interested in history, culture, outdoor activities, or arts and crafts, you are sure to find something to love in this beautiful city. So pack your bags, and come experience the magic of Cusco for yourself!
